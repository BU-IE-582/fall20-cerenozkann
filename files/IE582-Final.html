Based on some literature review it is found that several algortihm can be applied for bag-leveled classification approaches. Those are inclueding MIL-SVM(support-vector machine), MILAS, KNN etc. However, there are no sufficient resources for R applications. Hence, I find it suitable to use â€œkmeansâ€ clustering method in order to classify the bags.

To find the optimal number of bags, first a general tree is determined and the dendogram is plotted. From the plot, we can have some intuiton about the number of clusters. From dendogram, when the length of the lines is decreased, the line no in the steepest decrease can give some sense. Hence, to start, no of 10 clusters is chosen.

After clustering, we should classify the clusters according to the positive or negative classes. From the description, it seems that tree-based approaches (decision trees and random forest) is a good way to predict the classes.

As a general conclusion, DT method works better than RF for this dataser since the RMSE values are smaller for each try. Also, as we increase the number of clusters in kmeans algorithm, we get lower RMSE also, BUT this does not mean that many clusters are good. On the contrary, it may cause some over-fitting which is not desired. As a result, k=10 works a good classifier parameter for kmeans and generallt Decision Trees are good classifiers for this dataset.
